Executive Summary: Clean Energy Communities Re-Grant Program

Overview

The Clean Energy Communities (CEC) Re-Grant Program aims to provide financial support to Community-Based Organizations (CBOs) and Building Owners for implementing clean energy initiatives and energy efficiency upgrades.

Key Stakeholders

Regulated Entities

  • Community-Based Organizations: Eligible to apply for funding under the program.
  • Building Owners: Owners of buildings qualifying for energy efficiency funding.

Regulatory and Enforcement Authorities

  • Mayorâ€™s Office of Long-Term Planning and Sustainability (OLTPS): Proposes and oversees the program.
  • Steering Committee: Responsible for reviewing applications and awarding grants.

Other Stakeholders

  • Tenants: Residents benefiting from clean energy campaigns and efficiency upgrades.
  • Contractors: Organizations like ICF Resources, LLC. and Solar One administering the program.

Regulatory Framework

Regulation Title: Clean Energy Communities Re-Grant Program

Description: A funding initiative supporting energy efficiency projects and clean energy advocacy.

Legal Reference: Section 1043 of the New York City Charter, authorizing OLTPS to establish rules.

Eligibility and Compliance

Defined Conditions

  • Clean Energy Campaigns: Applicants must be a CBO operating in specified Community Districts.
  • Energy Efficiency Upgrades: Buildings must be affordable multifamily housing with five or more units and owned by a non-profit or owner-occupied.

Commitments & Obligations

  • Applicants must sign a Re-Grant Agreement to receive funding.
  • Awardees must submit project completion documentation for final payment.

Enforcement & Consequences

Violation Events

  • Failure to provide required documentation.
  • Not meeting program eligibility criteria.

Non-Compliance Consequences

  • Denial of funding for failure to comply with requirements.

Program Benefits

  • Funding up to $15,000 for Community-Based Organizations.
  • Funding up to $25,000 for eligible Building Owners.

Regulatory Relationships

  • OLTPS oversees and regulates the program.
  • Steering Committee enforces compliance and selects awardees.
  • Property Owners and CBOs must adhere to eligibility and application requirements.
  • Failure to comply results in denial of funding.

Conclusion

The Clean Energy Communities Re-Grant Program provides financial incentives to support sustainable energy initiatives. With clear **eligibility criteria, compliance requirements, and enforcement mechanisms**, the program ensures funding is directed towards impactful projects that benefit local communities.
